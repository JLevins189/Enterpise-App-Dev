Early Club Career

Messi came on in the 75th minute at just 16 years, 4 months and 23 days old against Porto in 2003. He impressed and began training with the reserve team Barcelona B daily and weekly with the first team. In his first training session he befriended Ronaldinho who knew he would become an even better player than he was.

His progress was reflected in his first professional contract, signed on 4 February 2004, which lasted until 2012.

Following pressure from the first team, Rijkaard promoted Messi to the first team in October 2004 As a substitute player, he played 244 minutes in nine matches for the first team that season, including his debut in the UEFA Champions League against Shakhtar Donetsk. He scored his first senior goal on 1 May 2005, against Albacete, from an assist by Ronaldinho, becoming at that time the youngest-ever scorer for the club. Barcelona, in their second season under Rijkaard, won the league for the first time in six years

On 24 June, his 18th birthday, Messi signed his first contract as a senior team player. He gradually established himself as the first-choice right winger, forming an attacking trio with Ronaldinho and striker Samuel Eto'o. While Barcelona began a gradual decline, the 19-year-old Messi established himself as one of the best players in the world during the 2006-07 campaign.

Current Situation

After many successful years at Barcelona, Messi's relationship with then club president Josep Bartomeu detoriated as the club began a steep decline from its very highs and financial troubles gripped the club. The financial issues forced Messi to have to move away.

Messi secured a move to PSG joining the most talented footballers in the world, Neymar and Mbappe. He failed to show the player everybody knew he was. However, in late 2022 his form improved, his PSG performances showed glimpses of his best despite his age.

Finally...

Following the heartbreak of losing to Germany in the World Cup final in 2014 and having his status of being the greatest of all time debated by the lack of international silverware, Messi led Argentina to a historic victory. Following their win in the CONMEBOL trophy, Messi led his team to the biggest trophy of all...
